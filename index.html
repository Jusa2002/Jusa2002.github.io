Who I am

About Me

I'm José Pedro, 23 years old, originally from Barcelos, Braga. I've always been a naturally curious person with a strong pull towards science and logic — the kind of person who needs to understand why something works before being satisfied with the answer.

That curiosity led me to fall in love with Mathematics in high school, and I decided to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics and Computation at Instituto Superior Técnico, one of the top engineering universities in Portugal. The programme was mathematically intensive and gave me a solid foundation in algorithms, data structures, and computational thinking. More than the technical knowledge, it sharpened my ability to reason carefully about problems — which I consider my strongest asset as an engineer.

My best characteristic is probably this: I have a good intuition for spotting where a process could be more efficient, and for anticipating why something might fail before it does. Whether it's a test suite, a codebase, or a workflow, I tend to look for the redundancy or the edge case others overlook.

After graduating, I joined Critical Software, where I work as a Software Tester embedded in the Hitachi Rail team on projects for Infraestruturas de Portugal. I test safety-critical interlocking software, handle ETCS parameter validation, and built an internal Java tool to automate parameter cross-checking and test database population — a project that significantly improved our team's validation throughput.

To deepen my grounding in computer science, I'm currently pursuing a Master's degree in Computer Science at ISCTE, which I attend in the evenings alongside my full-time role. I'm interested in software engineering, backend development, and the kinds of optimisation problems that sit at the intersection of mathematics and code.
